Question 1

[1014919] Administering of investments covers:
A
Settlement
B
Broking
C
Market making
D
Custody
The correct answer is: A - Settlement

Explanation

Administering covers services provided to the owner or manager of investments. This includes settlement (a regulated activity).

Question 2

[1014924] When managing investments, as per FSMA 2000, a person is:
A
Exercising discretion
B
Acting on instructions only
C
Exercising options
D
Acting as a custodian
The correct answer is: A - Exercising discretion

Explanation

When managing investments, a person exercises discretion over investments decisions. Acting as a custodian falls into the category of safeguarding and administering investments. Acting on instructions can apply to execution only services, not discretionary services.

Question 16

[1014935] There is a General Prohibition against carrying on investment business unless authorised or exempt. It is a defence for a person to show that he:
I
Took all reasonable precautions
II
Overlooked the requirement
III
Exercised due diligence
IV
Exercised his free will
A
I and II
B
I and III
C
II and III
D
III and IV
The correct answer is: B - I and III

Explanation

The General Prohibition requires that any person who undertakes investment business in the UK has to be authorised or exempt.
